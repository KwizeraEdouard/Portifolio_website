This book is about taking people from where they are now and helping them realise their dreams and goals – whatever they might be.

Awaken the Giant Within
by Tony Robbins

This book is aimed to be a tutorial for self-mastery and fulfillment by encourage readers to believe in themselves, take control of their lives, commit to taking consistent action, and manage their emotions, to name a few.

This book is about an emotional and mental attitude that focuses on the good and expects results that will benefit you. It's about anticipating happiness, health and success instead of expecting the worst

Barack Obama


After his parents divorced, Barack Obama's father left and returned to Kenya. According to Obama, he grew up as an Indonesian, a Hawaiian, a black, and a white child. Soon after, his mother moved him to Hawaii to live with his grandfather. Barack Obama was a competent student, but he wasn't exceptional.  Despite the fact that Obama was unable to live with his parents, he pursued his dream. Finally, he was elected president of a powerful country like the United States.


Perhaps you have a similar story. "Your life matters," Obama once said. Remember that even if you are unhappy with your life, you are the one who can make it shine by giving it meaning.

Orpah Gail Winfrey


She was born to a single teenage mother on welfare in rural Mississippi. She felt unwanted and was shuttled back and forth from her grandmother to her mother and then to her father by the time she was 14. She lived in poverty and suffered abuse for years.



She was also lonely much of the time and due to her family’s poverty, conditions were poor. But her grandmother taught her to read before she was three years old and she still recalls the positive reception she received when she recited Bible verses at her grandmother’s church. In fact, Oprah had to overcome many challenges and obstacles before achieving the success she enjoys today. She began life on a small farm in Mississippi where her strict grandmother raised her. Despite the hardship she become one of America’s most influential people and the first African-American billionaire, and yet it is.
